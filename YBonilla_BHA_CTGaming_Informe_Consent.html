What is the name of the study?

The name of the study is Clinical Telegaming Simulation.

What will you, as the patient volunteer, be doing throughout the study?

As a patient volunteer, you will be using an online gaming system that that evaluates movement and mood through a sensory online system.

What is the purpose for the study?

The purpose for the study is to evaluate motor function effects through the online gaming system.

Will the patient volunteer go through invasive procedures?

Patient volunteers will not be undergoing any type of invasive procedure in the study.

Will the patient go through pharmaceutical treatments?

Patient volunteers will not be given additional pharmaceutical treatments as part of the study. However, if patient volunteers involved in the study are currently taking pharmaceuticals for health conditions, the patient volunteers may continue to do so.

What are the risks of the study?

The risks of the study will be the slow and loss of internet connectivity at an unexpected time due to environmental fators that are out of the patient volunteer's control.

What are the benefits of the study?

The benefits of the study include being able to focus on the online game simulation once daily while contributing to the Brain Health Alliance team's primary intention to improve the brain health of all people.

What does the patient need to have for the study?

In order for the patient to be in the study, the patient volunteers need to commit once daily for the online gaming system while having a computer that allows the patient volunteer to complete the daily tasks on playing the onlien game simulation.

What will the patient volunteer expect from being part of the study?

The patient volunteer will be expected to have a computer or laptop that works well and works well with high speed internet, devote once daily to the online gaming simulation experince for a seven day period from the confort of their own home, and being able to follow up with the Brain Health Alliance throughout the study. The online gaming study will require the patient volunteer to press a button as a response to the action that is being requested by the online game simulation, such as focusing on an object and clicking on the object. The patients volunteers will not be interacting with the Brain Health Alliance team members at all, since this is a home virtual study

How will the study be delivered?

The study will be delivered via the Internet through the Brain Health Alliance website and the online game simulation website through a webpage. The team members from the Brain Health Alliance will not be in the patient volunteer's home in person

What will the patient volunteer have to do in the study?

The patient will need to spend once daily for seven days of the online gaming study from the beginning of the online gaming study to the end of the online gaming study while filling out a health questionnaire and a HIPAA form to protect the patient volunteer's information. The data provided by the patient volunteer in the questionnaires and the online game simulation will be saved on the web application.
